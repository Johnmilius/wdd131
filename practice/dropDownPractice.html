Best Places To Surf!

Location: North Shore, Oahu

Wave Type: Reef break

Pipeline is one of the most famous surf spots in the world, located on the North Shore of Oahu, Hawaii. The wave here is known for its powerful barrels that form over a shallow reef, creating hollow waves that are perfect for experienced surfers looking for a challenge. The wave can break anywhere from 6 feet to 20+ feet during the winter months, making it a top destination for big wave surfers. It’s often considered a proving ground for professional surfers, hosting the annual Billabong Pipeline Masters event as part of the World Surf League championship tour.

Location: Eastern Cape, South Africa

Wave Type: Point break

Jeffrey’s Bay is globally recognized for its long, fast, and incredibly smooth right-hand point breaks. It’s a top destination for surfers seeking consistent, world-class waves. The bay’s distinctive geography creates one of the longest rides in the world, with waves sometimes stretching over 300 meters. The spot is known for being particularly favorable in the Southern Hemisphere’s winter months, with waves often reaching overhead size. The area also hosts the World Surf League's J-Bay Open, attracting top-tier surfers and fans from all over the world.

Location: Island of Tahiti, French Polynesia

Wave Type: Reef break

Teahupo’o is infamous for having some of the thickest and heaviest waves on the planet. The reef break here creates powerful, fast barrels that can be daunting even for the most experienced surfers. What makes Teahupo’o unique is the way the wave breaks—closer to the shore than most other spots, over a shallow reef, creating massive, hollow tubes. It’s renowned for its extreme conditions, which makes it a mecca for big-wave surfers seeking a thrilling, world-class challenge. The spot is also known for its stunning beauty, with turquoise waters and dramatic tropical scenery.
